# Healthcare Data Analysis & Interactive Dashboard in Excel

## Project Objective

To process, clean, and analyze raw healthcare data, then build an interactive dashboard that visualizes key patient metrics, medical trends, and operational insights. The goal is to help users easily explore patterns, compare data across time periods, and make data-driven decisions regarding patient demographics, health conditions, admissions, and test outcomes.

## Dataset Used

[Healthcare_Dashboard.xlsx](https://github.com/jimilao0110-ps/Healthcare_Dashboard/blob/main/healthcare_dataset_raw.xlsx)

## Questions & Key Performance Indicators (KPIs)
### Core Questions
- What is the total number of patients and their average age?
- How does patient volume change across months and years?
- What are the most common medical conditions over time?
- How are admissions distributed between elective, emergency, and urgent cases?
- What is the distribution of blood types among patients?
- What percentage of test results are normal, abnormal, or inconclusive?
- What is the gender split of patients?
### KPIs
- Total Patients
- Average Age
- Monthly/Yearly Patient Count
- Medical Condition Trends (2019–2024)
- Admission Type Percentage
- Blood Type Distribution
- Test Result Breakdown
- Gender Ratio
## Process
1. **Data Cleaning**: Removed duplicates, fixed missing values, standardized formats, and ensured consistency across all fields.
2. **Data Structuring**: Organized raw data into formatted Excel tables, created helper columns (month, year, age groups), and established relationships between categories.
3. **Analysis**: Calculated totals, averages, percentages, and trends using formulas, pivot tables, and pivot charts.
4. **Dashboard Design**: Built an interactive interface with:
    - Key metrics cards
    - Line, bar, and pie charts
    - Filter controls (by month and year)
    - Dynamic visuals that update based on user selection

## Project Insights
- **Patient Volume**: Overall patient numbers remain stable year-round, with a noticeable drop in May; counts recovered and rose through the rest of the year.
- **Medical Conditions**: Hypertension and Obesity show the highest prevalence, followed closely by Diabetes; all conditions increased gradually from 2019 to 2023, with a slight decline in 2024.
- **Admissions**: Admission types are nearly evenly split, showing balanced workload across planned, emergency, and urgent care services.
- **Blood Types**: A+ and B+ are the most common; O- is the rarest in this dataset.
- **Test Results**: Only one-third of results are normal, meaning two-thirds of patients show abnormal or inconclusive findings — highlighting a high need for follow-up or further testing.
- **Demographics**: Gender distribution is perfectly balanced; average patient age is 52 years old, indicating a largely adult/middle-aged patient base.
## Final Conclusion
This project successfully transformed raw, unstructured healthcare data into a clear, interactive analytical tool. The dashboard reveals stable patient volume, rising chronic conditions, balanced admission patterns, and significant rates of abnormal test results. These insights can support healthcare planning, resource allocation, and targeted health programs. Excel proved highly effective for end-to-end processing, analysis, and visualization — making powerful data insights accessible without advanced tools.
