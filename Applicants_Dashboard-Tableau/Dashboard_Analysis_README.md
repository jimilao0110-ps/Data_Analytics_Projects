# Applicants Dashboard – Talent & Workforce Analytics
## Project Objective
The main goal of this project is to analyze and visualize key characteristics of 10,000 job applicants across multiple dimensions — including location, education, experience, skills, employment preferences, demographics, and work arrangements. It aims to provide clear, actionable insights to support talent acquisition, workforce planning, and recruitment strategy decisions.
## Dataset Used
[Applicants_Dataset](https://github.com/jimilao0110-ps/Data_Analytics_Projects/blob/main/Applicants_Dashboard-Tableau/Applicants_data.xlsx)
## Questions / Key Performance Indicators (KPIs)
1. Where are applicants located geographically?
2. What is the educational background distribution of candidates?
3. What are the preferred employment types and work arrangements?
4. Which technical fields have the most applicants?
5. How is experience distributed — both overall and within their field?
6. What is the gender distribution and average age of the talent pool?
7. Key KPIs:
    - Total applicants: 10,000
    - Average age: 31.64 years
    - Count and percentage breakdowns for all categories
## Process
1. **Data Collection**: Gather applicant data covering location, education, experience, skills, preferences, and demographics.
2. **Data Preparation**: Clean, validate, and structure the data; group and categorize values (e.g., experience levels, education tiers, tech fields).
3. **Aggregation**: Calculate totals, counts, averages, and proportions for each dimension.
4. **Visualization Design**: Design dashboard layout with charts and metrics to answer each analytical question clearly:
    - Bar charts: Location, education, experience
    - Pie charts: Employment type, gender
    - Tables: Tech fields, work arrangements
    - Key number cards: Total applicants, average age
5. **Insight Generation**: Interpret visual patterns and summarize findings.

## Project Insights
1. **Geography**: Major talent hubs are in the US (SF, NY, Seattle, Austin, Boston), Europe (London, Berlin, Amsterdam), and India (Bangalore, Hyderabad). Remote talent is also significant (546 globally).
2. **Education**: Bachelor’s and Master’s degrees are the most common qualifications; Bootcamp graduates form a large, skilled segment, showing strong alternative education pathways.
3. **Work Preferences**:
    - **Employment**: Strong preference for full-time roles.
    - **Arrangement**: Remote and Hybrid work are highly favored — together making up over 75% of preferences; on-site is less preferred.
4. **Skills & Tech**:
    - Most popular fields: Full-Stack Development, Data Science & ML, Backend, Frontend, DevOps/Cloud, Product Management.
    - Emerging fields like Blockchain & Web3 have smaller but notable representation.
5. **Experience**:
    - **Mid-level** talent dominates — both in total and field-specific experience — meaning the pool is rich in candidates with practical experience.
    - **Senior-level** talent is scarce, especially in field-specific expertise.
6. **Demographics**:
    - Average age is ~32, indicating a relatively young, active workforce.
    - Gender imbalance exists (male majority), with growing representation of other identities.
## Final Conclusion
This dashboard provides a comprehensive view of the talent pool, showing a **large, skilled, and experienced workforce** concentrated in major tech hubs and open to remote/hybrid work. Key takeaways for recruitment:
Focus sourcing in top locations and for remote roles.
Prioritize mid-level candidates — they form the bulk of available talent.
Strong demand exists for full-time roles and remote-friendly work policies.
High availability of candidates in core tech fields; niche/advanced roles (senior, specialized domains) will require more targeted sourcing.
Education diversity means both traditional degrees and bootcamp backgrounds produce strong candidates.
These insights help shape smarter sourcing strategies, role design, and workforce planning to align with candidate preferences and market availability.
